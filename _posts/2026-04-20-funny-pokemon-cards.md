The Pokémon TCG has been running for 30 years across thousands of unique illustrations. Most are serious &mdash; creatures in dynamic battle poses, holographic foil, dramatic lighting. But some are just *funny*. A Magikarp flopping miserably. A Slaking whose entire description is "it doesn't do anything." A Pokémon cooking ramen, napping in a box, riding a bicycle.

This post is a tour of the funniest Pokémon cards worth knowing about &mdash; the ones that get reshared online, made into memes, and collected specifically because they made someone laugh.

## The "what does this card even do?" tier

These are cards famous for flavor text or mechanics so absurd they became memes.

### Slaking (Jungle Relief, many reprints)

Slaking is the most famous example of joke design in Pokémon. Its ability, **Lazy**, has shipped in various forms &mdash; the most-quoted version being a flip-a-coin mechanic where Slaking usually does nothing on your turn. The Pokédex flavor text on several Slaking cards leans into it hard: "It spends the whole day lying down and lolling about." Competitive nightmare, comedy gold.

### Magikarp (Base Set, Jungle, many more)

The original joke Pokémon. Flavor text across Magikarp cards has described it as "virtually useless in battle," "flailing helplessly," and able only to use **Splash**, an attack that does zero damage. The card is a commitment to a bit that has run for three decades.

### Farfetch'd (Jungle)

Holding a leek. Every artist has leaned into the absurdity &mdash; Farfetch'd is usually depicted looking mildly annoyed, holding its vegetable sword like it's about to fight you with a spring onion. The Pokédex text on some prints genuinely implies it was nearly hunted to extinction because people wanted to eat the leek.

## Pokémon that are just inherently funny

Some Pokémon designs are jokes from the concept up. Every card of these is going to raise a smile.

- **Mr. Mime** &mdash; perpetually unsettling, every card tries to rehabilitate his image and fails.
- **Jynx** &mdash; lipstick, opera gloves, and absolutely zero chill.
- **Vanilluxe / Vanillite** &mdash; literal ice cream cones. "Running out of ideas" jokes wrote themselves.
- **Klefki** &mdash; a key ring. A key ring!
- **Trubbish / Garbodor** &mdash; bags of garbage. They are not subtle.
- **Polteageist / Sinistea** &mdash; a ghost that lives inside a teacup.
- **Applin / Flapple / Appletun** &mdash; a dragon made of apples. Apple dragon.
- **Yamask / Cofagrigus** &mdash; a Pokémon carrying a mask of its own human face, which is genuinely haunting when you think about it.
- **Hitmontop** &mdash; it spins. The whole design is it spins.

## The "Pokémon doing human things" illustration rares

The modern Scarlet &amp; Violet era introduced a new rarity called **Illustration Rare** / **Special Illustration Rare** where the art shows Pokémon in everyday scenes instead of battle poses. A huge amount of the best comedy in Pokémon TCG lives here.

Recurring gags:

- Pokémon **cooking** (Sinistea making tea, Slowpoke eating food, Appletun baking)
- Pokémon **shopping** or browsing a bookstore
- Pokémon **napping** in humanlike situations (in boxes, on couches)
- Pokémon **rain-soaked** at a bus stop
- Pokémon **arguing** with other Pokémon over snacks

The SWSH Battle Styles set onward leaned into this hard. The Scarlet &amp; Violet 151, Paldean Fates and Obsidian Flames sets doubled down. The whole **Trainer Gallery** subset in Brilliant Stars through Crown Zenith is built around warm, cozy, human-feeling scenes.

## "Fat Chu" and other meme cards

The Pokémon community has adopted a handful of cards as permanent meme canon:

- **Pikachu VMAX "Fat Chu"** (Vivid Voltage) &mdash; the round Pikachu that launched a thousand jokes. Perfectly canonical chonk. [See the set &rarr;](/sets/vivid-voltage/)
- **Giant Plant Forest** (various) &mdash; just a massive tree. Nothing else happens.
- **Magikarp & Wailord Tag Team** (Cosmic Eclipse) &mdash; a Magikarp and a Wailord on the same team. The size difference alone is the joke.
- **Unown** (various) &mdash; cards whose entire value is that Unown are just letters. Forming sentences with Unown cards is a whole sub-hobby.
- **Wobbuffet in Yuka Morii's clay style** &mdash; somehow both dignified and hilarious. (See our [Yuka Morii post](/blog/yuka-morii-pokemon-cards/) for more.)
- **Ditto transformed** cards &mdash; Ditto pretending to be other Pokémon with visibly wrong eyes.

## Trainer cards with questionable energy

Some of the funniest cards in the TCG aren't Pokémon at all &mdash; they're Trainer cards with bizarre art or flavor text.

- **Bill** (Base Set) &mdash; a man with shorts who hands you cards. Iconic, inexplicable.
- **Bill's Grandpa** (E-Card) &mdash; exactly what it sounds like.
- **Professor Oak's Research** across many sets &mdash; variations of a visibly tired old man.
- **Team Rocket Trainer cards** &mdash; increasingly desperate Team Rocket schemes documented card by card.
- **Healer** (various) &mdash; a woman standing quietly with a Chansey. The Chansey looks concerned about you.
- **Roxanne's Geologist Handbook** and other niche trainer supporters with deeply specific art.

## Error cards and printing quirks

Some "funny" cards aren't funny by design &mdash; they're funny because something went wrong at the printer:

- The **Shiny Charizard** with the missing shiny treatment (rare misprints).
- The **Ancient Mew** promo with various language layouts printed backwards.
- Various **Japanese-to-English mistranslations** on old cards.
- The **"no stage"** error cards where evolution stages were printed without the stage indicator.

Misprints can be worth real money in graded condition. [Our graded Pokémon cards guide](/blog/graded-pokemon-cards/) walks through why condition matters so much on error cards.

## Are any of these worth money?

Most funny cards aren't worth more than a few dollars &mdash; the humor doesn't usually create collector premiums on its own. The exceptions:

- **Fat Chu** (Vivid Voltage Pikachu VMAX): PSA 10 ~$200&ndash;$500
- **Moonbreon** (Evolving Skies Umbreon VMAX Alt Art): PSA 10 ~$2,000&ndash;$5,000+
- **Rayquaza VMAX Alt Art** (Evolving Skies): PSA 10 ~$1,000&ndash;$2,200
- Many **modern illustration rares** with Pokémon-doing-everyday-things art: $20&ndash;$150 for PSA 10 depending on Pokémon
- **Trophy / promo Trainer cards**: can reach four or five figures depending on rarity
